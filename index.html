Meray Maddah

PhD Candidate in Political Science  ·  University of Konstanz

I study what national legislators do beyond their home parliaments — in international organizations, transnational networks, and diplomatic settings — and what this tells us about the regimes they represent.

I'm particularly interested in autocracies and hybrid regimes, international parliamentary institutions, and legislative foreign engagement across the MENA region.

My background spans academic research and policy. Formerly a research fellow at SIPRI and a consultant for UNODC; most recently a visiting scholar at Harvard's Department of Government, hosted by Melani Cammett. I am also a Global Fellow at Brown University's Center for Human Rights and Humanitarian Studies.

Things I'm currently thinking about — reach out if you are too:

  • how autocracies engage with and exploit international parliamentary institutions
  • legislative foreign engagement and regime survival
  • gender and representation in the IPU
  • parliamentary friendship groups across MENA legislatures
